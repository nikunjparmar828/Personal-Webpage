About Me

My research is highly multidisciplinary in nature, combining elements from Robotics, Engineering design, Computational engineering, Computer Programming, and Material Science. My recent work involves the design and development of Intelligent Robots.

Recently, I got an opportunity to work with the R&D team at Morphle Labs Pvt. Ltd. as a Robotics Research Intern. I have performed a number of experiments on various control boards, DC motors, Motor Drivers, Linear, and Rotational encoders, and firmware like MARLIN and GRBL to find out the best combination for our whole slide microscope image scanners.

From Jan-May 2020, I completed my final-year project (Internship) from the Institute for Plasma Research (IPR), Gandhinagar. I was working on the Design and Prototype Development of a Multi-DOF Parallel Haptic Arm. In the summer of 2019, I got an opportunity to work at Sea Tech Lab, Florida Atlantic University, FL, USA as a research scholar. I did research on optimal path planning of Unmanned Surface Vehicles in dynamic environments. I was guided by Dr. Manhar Dhanak and Armando Sinnisterra.

I’m also interested in Mechanical Product designing and Automation. In my sophomore year, I worked at Tirth Agro Technology Pvt. Ltd. as a Trainee.
