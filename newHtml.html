In-Helmet Spacesuit Device

Undergraduate Project Dr. Ankur Pandya · Nirma University

During EVAs, substances such as sweat, irritants, moisture, coolant, antifogging agents from the helmet visor etc. may interact with the astronauts’ eyes and facial region, causing debilitating pain, discomfort and also temporary loss of vision in some cases. The design of a device is to be attached inside spacesuit helmets, composed of sweeping robotic arms with absorbent and cleaning heads which would effectively remove the undesirable substances and provide relief, hence enabling the astronauts to operate unhindered and undistracted during spacesuit activities.

Extrusion Die

Academic Project Prof. Nilesh Ghetiya · Nirma University

Developed a Square face Extrusion die with use of Cast Iron. It can be used to extrude Ductile materials like Aluminium, Copper etc. We have Successfully developed the final product which is compatible with Industrial product. Successfully tested on materials like Wax and Aluminum. We Did a study on effects of Extrusion pressure, Die angle, Material’s flow from die’s outlet etc and also done Computational Fluid Dynamics(CFD) Analysis of Extrusion process. It was a group project
